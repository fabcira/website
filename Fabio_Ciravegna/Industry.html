Collaboration with Industry


Me during a recent visit at Tata Steel in Scunthorpe, for the Speak-PC project
I have more than 25 years of experience in collaborating with industry. I started my career as researcher at the Fiat Research Centre in Turin. Since then I have worked in academic research centres and academia but I have always kept collaborating with industry both as part of directly funded projects and as part of national and European projects. I have also co-created two companies, one of which has now a hundred thousands high value customers.
About 10% of the funds I have received since 2005 come either from direct funding from industry or from projects partly funded by industry (e.g. via Innovate UK/TSB funds). Since in Sheffield, major direct funders of my research have been Rolls-Royce plc (jet engines) and more recently JustGiving.

Interested in a collaboration?

If you or your company are interested in collaborating with me and my group, there are many ways; typically:
  • Consultancy: this is typically for short activities (up to some weeks) to solve one specific problem or to provide tutoring. For SMEs there are funds that can help from either the government (e.g. innovation vouchers) or the University.
  • Innovation Projects: these are typically longer term (e.g. some months up to a couple of years) and have typically a wider perspective than consultancy. They can be funded by the company itself or Innovate UK/TSB, the European Commission, etc.
  • Research projects: are typically long term (some years) for basic and applied research, funded again either by the company or InnovateUK/TSB or the European Commission.
Often we have also blue skies research projects (e.g. funded by the EPSRC) and we seek informal collaboration (e.g. case studies) in industry. Also letters of support for our funding applications are very welcome. I am always happy to discuss about potential collaborations without commitment on either side; feel free to contact me.
