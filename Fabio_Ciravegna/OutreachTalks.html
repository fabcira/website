Outreach: Schools

These are the outlines of the outreach talks that I am currently proposing to schools.

Are you preparing for a Digital World?

This is a motivational talk to describe the evolution of the digital world and to trace its past, present and future directions. The goal is to help children focus on their future career.
  • What is the digital world?
    • Computers and the Internet: where did they come from and where will they go?
    • Why do we have laptops and mobile phones?
    • The fundamental laws of the digital world: Moore’s law, the law of broadband evolution and the disk space evolution law
    • What computers will we have in 10 years' time?
    • From mobile devices to wearable computing: the devices of the future via tablets and Google Glass
  • The impact of the digital world
    • From social studies to medicine to law and engineering to your house
    • Facebook, Twitter, YouTube and Instagram: just cool apps to chat with your friends?
    • Why they are important and why you should use them
    • Why you should be careful when you use them
    • How they impact society and how they can be used to understand society: from social studies, to the response to large scale emergencies to managing Music festivals
  • Are you preparing for the future digital world?
    • Society, people and technology: how you can contribute to the future world
    • What you should know to prepare for a future job: are you learning the right material? Should you learn computing?
    • Why you should understand and learn to use digital technologies no matter if you will be an engineer or a historian How to learn computing on your own

Jets, Music Festivals, and Flying Drones. And you thought computing was boring?

This talk is aimed at students who may consider studying computing. It tries to dispel the idea that if you study computing you will end up - like Dilbert - working in a cubicle. It shows instead how a computer scientist is actually a problem solver that can be involved in amazing projects. The title comes from the topic of projects I am involved in and that I plan to describe to the students.
  • Computers and the Internet: where did they come from and where will they go?
    • Why do we have a laptops and mobile phones?
    • The fundamental laws of the digital world: Moore’s law, The law of broadband evolution and the disk space evolution law
    • What computers will we have in 10 year’s time?
    • From mobile devices to wearable computing: the devices of the future via tablets and Google Glass
  • Computer Scientists and Software Engineers: Dilbert-like freaks?
    • Working with the Web, Facebook. Twitter and Youtube: carnivals and music festivals
    • Monitoring the environment: Mobile phones, sensors and flying drones
    • Computer Scientists as problem solvers: Working with Jet engines
  • Learning computing
    • Why you should learn computing even if you plan to become a historian
    • How to learn computing on your own
    • Do you need to be a freak to learn computing?
